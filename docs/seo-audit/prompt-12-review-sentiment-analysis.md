__THE LANGUAGE GAP__

The clearest difference between how the top competitor's customers talk about their experience and how On Point Installations currently describes itself is this: customers use outcome and relief language, and On Point uses capability and category language\. Preferred Office Installations' best reviewer said: "I have never had an installation team that completed their job without requiring me to hold their hand\." That sentence describes a specific, felt outcome — the relief of not having to babysit a crew\. On Point's GBP currently says: "OPI is a full service office furniture installation company\. We provide a team of dedicated, qualified personnel who work in synergy to successfully solve your installation challenges\." Nobody who has hired On Point Installations has ever described their experience using those words\. The people who hired On Point said things like "trusted partner," "changes on the fly," "daily reports with pictures," "left the site pristine," and "I selfishly don't want to recommend them\." The marketing says none of this\.

The single most powerful phrase to add to every marketing asset immediately is: "The crew that solves problems without being asked\." This is a direct translation of the most\-feared scenario in the target buyer's mind — the crew that needs constant supervision — converted into a positive claim using the exact sentiment On Point's own reviews already prove\. Colleen Chambers wrote it in plain language: "whenever an issue arises, they promptly provide a solution without hesitation\."
